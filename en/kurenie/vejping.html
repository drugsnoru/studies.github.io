Vaping is when you use a portable electronic device to inhale fog ("steam") into your lungs. An electronic cigarette, vape pen, or other electronic nicotine delivery system (ENDS) heats liquid from nicotine, flavoring, propylene glycol, and other additives into the aerosol you inhale through the mouthpiece. Vaping can cause respiratory problems, organ damage, addiction and other conditions.

What is vaping?


Vaping is when you use a small, portable device (such as an electronic cigarette, vape pen, or fashion) to inhale vapors of nicotine and a flavoring agent (e-liquid). It's like smoking a cigarette, but vaping heats the smallest particles of liquid, not burning tobacco.

How does vaping work?


Vaping works by heating the liquid in a small device so you can breathe it into your lungs. An electronic cigarette, vape pen, or other vaping device heats the liquid in the device, creating an aerosol. It's not water vapor. E-cigarette vapor contains particles of nicotine, flavors and other substances suspended in the air. You inhale these particles into your mouth through the mouth mouth, from where they enter your throat and lungs.

What are e-cigarettes (vape pens)?


An electronic cigarette (ES) is a device that heats liquid nicotine and a flavor to be inhaled. There are many varieties of e-cigarettes that have different names, including vapes, vape pens or sticks, e-hookahs, hookah sticks, fashion, and personal vaporizers (PVs). They can also be called electronic nicotine delivery systems (ENDS).

Most types of e-cigarettes have


✔ Cartridge, tank or capsule containing liquid (can be refillable).
✔ Heating element to turn liquid into breathable particles (aerosol, commonly called "steam").
✔ Battery to power the heating element.
✔ Power or control button (some are activated by sensors upon inhalation).
✔ Mouthpiece for aerosol inhalation
.
What is the difference between vaping and smoking cigarettes?

Vaping and smoking involve inhaling nicotine and other substances into the lungs. E-cigarettes heat liquid to create aerosol; cigarettes burn tobacco to create smoke.

Is vaping worse than cigarettes?


Vaping is often considered safer than smoking cigarettes, but vaping also causes health problems. Both vaping and smoking are addictive and inject potentially dangerous chemicals into your body. Many of these chemicals are elevated when you burn tobacco. Vaping does not last long enough to know what long-term damage it can cause.

How does vaping affect the lungs?


The particles you inhale while vaporizing can cause inflammation (edema) and irritation in the lungs. This can damage the lungs, such as scarring and narrowing of the tubes that carry air into and out of the lungs. Researchers do not yet know all the effects of vaping on your body.

Isn't vaping just water vapor?


No. Despite the name, vaping does not create water vapor. In fact, it creates an aerosol (or fog) containing small particles of nicotine, metal and other harmful substances.

What is included in the composition of an electronic liquid (juice)?

An electronic liquid, also called an electronic juice or vape juice, is what vaping devices use to create the vapor that you inhale. Electronic fluids are more than just water.
