As the intoxication progresses, coordination and balance may become impaired, resulting in stumbling or clumsiness. Slurred speech, poor judgment, and difficulty concentrating or remembering are also common signs of intoxication.

In severe cases of intoxication, loss of consciousness or memory lapses may occur. It is important to note that the specific behavior of a drunk person may vary greatly depending on factors such as the person’s tolerance level, the amount and type of alcohol consumed, and other personal characteristics.

Signs and symptoms of alcohol intoxication in the Black people

Alcohol has different effects on each person. When a person drinks alcohol, it quickly enters their blood and reaches their brain within five minutes. As people consume more and more, the concentration of alcohol in the blood (BAC) rises, and the effects become more pronounced. Here are some of the most common signs of alcohol intoxication.


✔ Speech - A person who is intoxicated often slurs words. Alcohol can also make a person loud, aggressive, and even controversial.

✔ Movement is one of the most vivid signs of alcohol intoxication. Alcohol causes a loss of coordination. A person may rock their body, fumble around, or be timid with another person.

✔ Vision - Alcohol usually makes the eyes become shiny and red. Sometimes the eyes begin to drop, roll, or even close.

✔ Condemnation. This symptom may be a little harder to identify if you personally do not know a drunk person, but you can usually tell when someone is acting abnormally.

✔ Appearance - The general appearance of a person in a state of intoxication changes. The first three things we talked about, movement, speech, and vision, are going to be turned off. A person may have difficulty keeping himself upright, speech may be disrupted, and, as noted above, their eyes will usually be glass.


Alcohol ingestion causes a large amount of norepinephrine to be released into the body. Noradrenaline is a natural stimulant produced by the body. When large amounts of norepinephrine are released, braking decreases and the person becomes more impulsive. Alcohol is also a depressant. It depresses the brain, which affects a person's ability to make rational decisions.
