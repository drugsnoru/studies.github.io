Drugs are increasingly being mixed with dangerous synthetic opioids, such as nitazenes, that have killed hundreds of people worldwide. It happens with a wide range of drugs, not just heroin, and you can never know for sure what is in any street or fake drug.

Synthetic opioids

are artificial drugs that mimic the effects of natural opioids (such as opium or heroin) but can be much more deadly. Most synthetic opioids, including nitazenes and fentanyl, are class A drugs, which means that they are illegal to own, distribute or sell. Some synthetic opioids are used in public health as pain relievers or anesthetics, but counterfeits are also produced and sold illegally. Others have no legal application and are manufactured and sold only illegally.


Synthetic opioids are often mixed with other drugs, particularly heroin, which may increase the risk of an overdose. They have also been found in some counterfeit tablets, especially benzodiazepines such as diazepam, and opioid painkillers such as oxycodone, as well as other illicit drugs such as synthetic cannabinoids, cocaine and ecstasy. Don’t be fooled by a package that looks like a real medicine—just because a medicine looks like a real medicine doesn’t mean it is.

Overdose with synthetic opioids is very simple, because there is only a small difference between the dose that will cause a high and the dose that can kill you.

The opioid overdose antidote, naloxone, acts on synthetic opioids, so keep it at hand if you're going to put yourself at risk. You can get it at the nearest drug treatment center.

Start with a small amount and wait to see the effect before taking more.

Do not use alone.

If you're in a group, don't use drugs all at once, so there's no one who can help and get help if people cross the threshold.
