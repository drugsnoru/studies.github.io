What is Ewali?


Evali is a serious lung disease caused by vaping (smoking e-cigarettes). Vitamin E acetate, contained in certain electron fluids, is a possible cause.
The Evali outbreak in late 2019 and early 2020 sent thousands to hospital. At least 68 people died. Since then, cases of EVALI have declined, but people who smoke vapes can still become ill with Evali.
Among those hospitalized with severe Evali, most were under 35 years old and used THC-containing vapes from unofficial sources (online, family, or friends). However, EVALI can occur in anyone using both nicotine and THC-containing vapes.
